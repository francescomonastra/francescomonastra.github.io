I'm a PhD student in Astrophysics at INAF-IAPS / ASI SSDC, working on high-energy X-ray instrumentation and detector simulations for future space missions. I started my PhD in 2025, with a project titled *"Use of Artificial Intelligence for Particle Background Subtraction in X-ray Astronomy"* — combining Geant4 simulations and machine learning to understand and suppress the particle background in X-ray detectors, mainly in the context of the NewAthena X-IFU instrument.

Before that, I completed my Master's degree in Astronomy and Astrophysics at Sapienza University of Rome, with a thesis titled <a href="/files/Tesi_Magistrale-FM.pdf" target="_blank"><em>Geant4 Simulations of Transition-Edge Sensors Detectors for the Large X-ray Observatory ATHENA and the Solar Axion Experiment IAXO</em></a>.

You can find more on my research interests on the [Research](/research/) page, and my work so far on the [Publications](/publications/) page.

Outside of the lab, I spend as much time as I can outdoors — hiking and climbing are my usual way of disconnecting (and, occasionally, of finding a good spot to think about physics with a better view).

Feel free to [get in touch](/contact/) if you'd like to talk about X-ray instrumentation, background simulations, or good climbing routes.
